Definition:
	An HR Clerk is an employee assigned to the HR Department and whose primary 
	job duty is to work directly with employee records.   The HR Clerk will also
	assist with general administrative tasks in the HR Department, including 
	scheduling and working with the hiring process.
	
Types of HR Clerk:
	At our sample organization, there is only one kind of HR Clerk.
	
	On rare occasion, a temporary worker or employee from another department 
	may take on the role of HR Clerk, but at that time, they will perform the 
	same basic data entry and administrative tasks as the HR Clerk, though they 
	may not participate in some of the department functions (such as hiring, 
	firing, etc.)

How does a person become an HR Clerk?
	Persons permanently assigned the position of HR Clerk go through the normal
	hiring process and meet all the requirements.  On hiring, the I. T. staff
	assigns all needed permissions for necessary systems.
	
	Temporary employees and employees borrowed from other departments will be 
	given permissions as needed by the I. T. staff.

How does a person stop being an HR Clerk?
	Permanently assigned HR Clerks stop being an HR Clerk when the assume 
	another position within the organization or when their employment ends.
	
	Temporary employees stop being HR Clerks upon the end of their contract.
	
	Borrowed employees stop being an HR Clerk when there is no longer need to 
	borrow them from other departments.
	
	When anyone stops being an HR Clerk, the I. T. staff removes the relevant 
	permissions.
	
Can a person occupy more than one role?
	It is possible for an employee to hold more than one position, one of which 
	could be HR Clerk.
	
	An HR Manager can perform all the tasks of an HR Clerk, but would be acting 
	as an HR Clerk at that time.

What do you need to know about a person who performs this role?
	An HR Clerk must fill the same identifying forms as all employees.  In 
	addition, they must have at least a high school or equivalent education, 
	with college preferred.  It is best that they have prior experience with 
	Human Resources, but can be trained.  Additionally, they must show skill 
	in the following tasks:
	-	Common office processes: filing, mail handling, telephone procedures
	-	Using computers including office productivity tools 
	-	Spoken and written communication with internal and external clients
	-	Multitasking and attention to details 
	-	Reliability and trustworthiness

Why is the role called what it is?
	'HR' is a common abbreviation for 'Human Resources', which is the typical 
	name for the department in an organization that manages employee records 
	and processes.
	'Clerk' is a traditional name for an employee who works with records and 
	office administrative tasks.  The word derives from the word "cleric"
	meaning "clergyman", the people who knew how to read and write in prior 
	centuries.  See https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clerk
